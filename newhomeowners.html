The Association's Purpose

The Windemere Farms Homeowners Association, Inc. was formed in 2002 and is governed by a board of volunteer directors. The charter is to improve property values while increasing the quality of life for the residents of the community. The Board of Directors is elected by the community to handle the daily functions and financial responsibilities of the Association. They oversee social events, landscaping, architectural control, communications, and welcoming new homeowners to the community. The Board is also responsible for maintaining members' compliance with the established covenants and bylaws.

  • Newsletters are published quarterly to keep residents informed about their community.
  • Community Garage Sales are held twice a year in the Spring and Fall seasons.
  • Social Events are conducted throughout the year which include activities like movie nights and food trucks. You can keep up with these dates on our Facebook page.
  • The Architectural Control Committee enforces the restrictive covenants. The association retains a legal service to assist in all covenants matters.

Services

  • Cable and Phone: While not included in dues, the community has upgraded telecommunications service from both AT&T and Spectrum Communications, offering a choice of state-of-the-art digital television and (Internet) data communications.
  • Water: services are provided by Evansville Water and Sewer Utiliity and paid by each resident.
  • Electric: services are provided by CenterPoint Energy and paid by each resident.
  • Trash: Renewable Resources LLC is our preferred trash company. They offer the HOA a discounted rate. They can be reached at 1.877.752.3024 or through the company website renewable-resources.org.

Annual Dues

Annual membership dues are currently $215 and are due on the 1st of each calendar year.
These funds help maintain the retention pond, streetlights, mowing of the common area, snow removal from the streets, and many other expenses.

Architectural Control Committee

As a new homeowner, you may already have some ideas on how you'd like to improve your property. Please keep in mind, the Association has an Architectural Control Committee to help maintain the quality of our neighborhood. Its function is to evaluate the plans for all exterior landscaping, repairs, and remodeling to ensure compliance with all Covenants and Bylaws. The purpose of this review is to protect our scenic environment and maintain the value of our homes. All modifications to the exterior of your property must be submitted to the ACC for approval before any work begins.
